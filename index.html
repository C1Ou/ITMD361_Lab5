Solar system concept

The solar system is centered on the sun, and all star systems of celestial bodies bound by the sun's gravity: 8 planets, 173 known moons, 5 dwarf planets and tens of thousands of small solar system objects. These small bodies contain asteroids, Kuiper Belt objects, comets and interstellar dust.

Solar system contains

The solar system includes: the sun, 4 terrestrial inner planets, many small rocky asteroid belts, 4 gas-filled giant outer planets and frozen small rocks, the second small celestial region called the Kuiper Belt. Outside the Kuiper belt, the ecliptic discrete disk and heliosphere belong to the Oort cloud.

The planetary extensions within the solar system are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Planets in the outer solar system are also surrounded by planetary rings made of dust and many small grains. Except for the earth, the Chinese names of the planets visible to the naked eye on the earth are named after five elements.
